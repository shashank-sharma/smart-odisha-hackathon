What Does Cyberbullying Look Like?


    Cyberbulling is an evil that appears in many forms, including:

  • Abusive text messages and emails.

  • Forceful exclusion from social groups.

  • Online humiliation.

  • Online impersonation and frapping.

  • Trolling which is against moral rights.

  • Illegal and forceful entry to sensitive data without your permission.

    • I am being Cyberbullied - How can I stop it?


    Know what you are looking for

    Always be on the lookout for fraping, cyberstalking, harrassment and impersonation. Cyberbullies do not attack with a notice. An person can be victimised anywhere and anytime. It does not even matter if the victim and the bully are acquainted or not.
    It is therefore important to know and understand the different types of cyberbullying.

    To know more about the different types of cyberbulling, visit: Types of Cyberbullying

    Take immediate action

    Attempt to identify the cause, stop responding to the bully's message. Save evidence in the form of screenshots or recordings before blocking the bully. After blocking the bully, do not forget to change your account settings.
    We will provide you with brief counseling and you can also submit your complaint.

    To know more, visit: Report Complaint

    Get outside help

    Always remeber: Do not hesitate and wait too long to ask for help. This can lead to disastrous situations. If you are a child or a teenager, ask an adult for help. Often, talking it out with parents and teachers is the best solution. You can also report the bully to your service providers if he is taking the telecommunication way to bully you. If the problem is too large to be handled, then it is best to get the law enforcement involved. This is where this portal comes into play.
    In case you have any doubt regarding the web portal, visit: FAQ Pagse

    Facets of the website


    • Complaint registry:
      The user can input his or her problem in the portal's complaint section. The input will be in the form of a text, describing the problem he or she is facing and against whom.
      • This will give a more profound evidence of when, how, where and by whom the victim was bullied.
    • Ticket generation:
      A ticket will be generated for every complaint. This ticket will possess a unique ID. This will help the consultant and the user to uniquely identify the complaint and track down the progress made by the officials in solving the issue.
    • Tags:
      Complaints will be classified on the basis of tags. This allows the officials to search for problems on the basis of tags.
      • This will include a certain level of flexibility to the search process and the officials as well since they can now search for tags in their area of expertise.
    • Data analysis:
      Data will be analyzed on a regular basis and shown in the analytics dashboard.
      • Analytical data will include the location(s) and the tag(s) associated with the complaint
      This will help in identifying the areas which are infested with the heinous crime of cyberbullying
      This will also bring into our notice the types of cyberbullying occuring in those areas, including the frequency at which the complaints are addressed and solved by the authorities.
    • Messaging system:
      Email/sms will be sent with third party modules.
